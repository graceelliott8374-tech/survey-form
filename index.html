Should Gender-Affirming Medical Care Legally Be Denied To Minors?

In recent years, hundreds of thousands of children and teens in the US have shared that their gender identity does not align neatly with the physical sex characteristics they were born with. The understanding that gender is on a spectrum, that one's gender and sex assigned at birth may not correspond, and that one doesn't need to fit into a particular stereotype, is a much more dominant viewpoint now than it was even a few years ago. This has caused a lot of people to pause and think more critically about who they are - not who they were told they are or who they think they should be. It has also caused a lot of controversy in US society.

🗣Legislation:

Recently, a wave of legislation and orders have emerged from conservative state houses designed to block gender-affirming medical treatment for young people.

In February of 2022, Texas Governor Greg Abbott issued a letter to the Department of Family and Protective Services directing the state agency to investigate medical treatments for transgender adolescents - such as puberty blockers and hormones - as child abuse . The letter asserts that there are reporting requirements forall licensed professionals who have contact with children who may be subject to such abuse, including doctors, nurses, and teachers, and that a failure to report merits criminal penalties.

As of now, 26 states have put forward anti-trans bills. Ohio hopes to enact Save Adolescents From Experimentation Act, modeled after similar legislation in Arkansas, which would ban access to gender-affirming care for transgender minors.
